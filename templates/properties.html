Properties of Aluminum Wires

Electrical Conductivity

Aluminum has about 61% of copper's electrical conductivity but is much lighter, making it ideal for overhead power lines. Conductivity is affected by purity and temperature, with high-purity aluminum (e.g., AA-1350) offering optimal performance.

Thermal Conductivity

Aluminum wires efficiently dissipate heat, suitable for high-temperature environments like transformers and motors. Their thermal conductivity supports applications requiring heat management.

Mechanical Properties

  • Tensile Strength: Varies by alloy, typically 150-300 MPa for electrical grades.
  • Ductility: Allows aluminum to be drawn into thin wires without breaking.
  • Fatigue Resistance: Suitable for dynamic applications like automotive wiring.

Chemical Properties

Aluminum forms a natural oxide layer, providing excellent corrosion resistance. This makes it ideal for outdoor and marine environments. However, care must be taken to prevent galvanic corrosion when in contact with other metals.

Physical Properties

  • Density: 2.7 g/cm³, much lighter than copper (8.9 g/cm³).
  • Melting Point: Approximately 660°C, suitable for most wiring applications.
  • Thermal Expansion: Higher than copper, requiring consideration in design.

Environmental Impact

Aluminum is highly recyclable, with recycled aluminum requiring only 5% of the energy needed for primary production. Its sustainability makes it a preferred choice for eco-conscious projects.
